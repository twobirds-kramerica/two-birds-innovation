Mission

Two Birds Innovation builds digital tools that improve access to information for Canadians who are typically underserved by technology — seniors navigating benefits and digital safety, job seekers needing structured AI assistance, and small businesses uncertain about where AI fits in their operations.

The work is Canadian-first: content reflects Canadian programmes, Canadian law, and Canadian communities. Products are publicly accessible, free to use, and built to run without ongoing cloud costs or vendor lock-in.

Technology Philosophy

Sovereignty-first architecture. All products are built as static HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no server, no database, no recurring cloud costs. This is a deliberate architectural choice: products can run on any static hosting platform, cannot be taken offline by a vendor decision, and cost nothing to operate at scale.

No data collection without purpose. Products that collect data (quiz results, job search history) store it exclusively in the user's own browser. Data does not leave the device. No user accounts, no email lists, no analytics dashboards.

Canadian content, Canadian law. Guides reflect CRA programmes, PIPEDA requirements, Canadian market conditions, and Ontario-specific benefits. This is not a US product adapted for Canada — it was built for Canada from the start.

Modular and maintainable. Each product is a standalone codebase. Updates to one product do not affect others. A single developer can maintain all products without build pipelines, package managers, or framework upgrades.

  • AI for All Strategy (Canada, June 2026) — AI literacy track for adults and youth, Canadian context, outcome-measured delivery, accessible to communities without existing AI infrastructure
  • SR&ED (Scientific Research and Experimental Development) — novel methodology for digital literacy outcome measurement, zero-PII data architecture, AI-assisted systematic review systems
